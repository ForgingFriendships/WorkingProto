Mission Statement


The mission of Forging Friendships is to create a social learning experience where typical children can meet new friends in a safe, non-oriented atmosphere. Children with and without disabilities learn from each other and everyone gains friendship.


Kindness Ambassadors

What differentiates Forging Friendships is the role that traditional children will play as volunteers. The traditional children would take part in our Empathy in Action and Fe Leadership ™ programs to become certified as Kindness Ambassadors ™.

The ambassadors will work as a team to help spearhead each Forging Friendship Event, building leadership and project management skills all while establishing meaningful friendships along the way.

Empathy in Action

The Empathy in Action program is designed to better understand what it means and how it feels to be a young person with a disability and addresses the overall fears and misconceptions about children with intellectual and physical disabilities.

This educational program provides the opportunity to learn what to say and how to best ask questions to a disabled peer, resulting in a positive interaction.

This programs works to create a conscious shift in the way special needs children are viewed by empowering the traditional child with tangible, real-life experiences.

Fe Leadership

The Fe Leadership program recognizes the need for kid-friendly leadership skills. This program covers introductory business and project management concepts that can translate into the children’s schools, churches, and communities.

What is Fe?

Fe is shorthand for iron. And we believe that our young leaders are pretty much super-hero’s, hence the “iron” part.
